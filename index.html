Pixel-GRM: Pixel-aligned Gaussian Reconstruction Model for 3D Reconstruction and Generation


Abstract

We introduce Pixel-GRM, a Generalizable Pixel-aligned Gaussian Reconstruction Model capable of reconstructing high-quality 3D assets from sparse-view images in ~0.2s. Pixel-GRM employs a simple, feed-forward transformer-based architecture to translates input images into per-pixel Gaussians directly to represent 3D scenes. By effectively leveraging the synergy between the transformer architecture and the pixel-aligned Gaussian representation, Pixel-GRM yields both effective and efficient 3D reconstruction. Extensive experimental results highlight the advantages of Pixel-GRM over existing state-of-the-art methods in terms of both reconstruction quality and speed. Remarkably, it sets a new benchmark with a PSNR of 31.38dB on the GSO dataset, marking a 17.6% improvement over the previous best result, while also being nearly 25x faster. Furthermore, we showcase the potential of Pixel-GRM in text-to-3D and image-to-3D generation, by combining it with existing multi-view diffusion models.

Pixel-GRM enables high-quality and efficient 3D Gaussian reconstruction from sparse-view images, with high-fidelity novel view renderings, and mesh extraction. Moreover, it can work in synergy with existing multi-view generators for text-to-3D and image-to-3D generation.


Method

Given sparse posed images, Pixel-GRM regresses pixel-aligned Gaussian parameters with a transformer-based reconstruction model. We un-project these pixel-aliened Gaussians along the ray directions to form a set of 3D Gaussians that represent the 3D asset, with which we can render high-quality novel-views efficiently.
